# Cryptic clues to Scottish islands 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿

It’s April Fools’ Day, or [April Cools’ Day](https://www.aprilcools.club/) if you prefer.
This is a good day for me to post something a little unexpected to my blog, that might surprise or (hopefully) amuse people.

I’ve been getting into cryptic crosswords!
I wouldn’t say I’m great at them — the number of full-size puzzles I’ve solved without looking up words is exactly two.
But my daily newspaper prints miniature ones, which I have a good chance of solving, and I can make a decent fist at the full-size ones.

So in that spirit, I’ve devised some clues, on the theme of islands in Scotland because there’s a lot of them.

A normal crossword might contain the clue <samp>Exhaust drug for Scottish island (5)</samp>, where <samp>Exhaust drug</samp> is wordplay and <samp>Scottish island</samp> is the definition.

However, all of the solutions below are Scottish islands, and I wanted to avoid repeating “Scottish island” (or suchlike) in every clue.
Therefore a clue below might have <em>two</em> wordplay components, instead of one wordplay and one definition (or indeed, two definitions).

So my clues are more like <samp>Exhaust drug, one in plant (5)</samp>, giving <q>Tiree</q>.
If you’re not used to cryptic clues, here’s a hint: <samp>exhaust drug</samp> can be interpreted as <q>tire E</q> (ecstasy), and <samp>one in plant</samp> can be interpreted as <q>I</q> (1) inside <q>tree</q>; hence the clue produces <q>Tiree</q> twice.
(I know this example clue is kinda rubbish, that’s why it’s the example.)

As well as synonyms, there may be anagrams, homophones, or other wordplay.
